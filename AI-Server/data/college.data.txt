Introduction to our college and Institutional Background

JSS Science and Technology University (JSS STU) is the second university established by JSS Mahavidyapeetha. The other university established by the organization is JSS Academy of Higher Education and Research, which is a deemed university focused on health sciences and located in Mysuru.

JSS STU plays an important role in providing high-quality skilled manpower in the fields of science, engineering and technology, and management. The university focuses on transforming society through research, innovation, and education.

JSS STU is committed to delivering quality education at all academic levels. It aims to provide opportunities for aspiring youth to meet the global needs of society across various sectors through knowledge, skills, and research-driven learning.

Sri Jayachamarajendra College of Engineering (SJCE)

Sri Jayachamarajendra College of Engineering, commonly known as SJCE, was established in the year 1963. It is one of the prominent engineering institutions associated with JSS Science and Technology University.

SJCE offers twelve engineering departments and also has a Master of Computer Applications department. The college supports technical education across multiple engineering disciplines.

Sri Jayachamarajendra College of Engineering was previously affiliated with Visvesvaraya Technological University, Belgaum. From the academic year 2016–2017 onwards, it became a constituent college of JSS Science and Technology University.

Sri Jayachamarajendra College of Engineering is accredited by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), ensuring compliance with national standards for technical education in India.

Research and Innovation Overview

Research and innovation form a core academic focus at JSS Science and Technology University. Rooted in the academic legacy of Sri Jayachamarajendra College of Engineering, Mysuru, the university is committed to advancing education and research in Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics, and Management.

JSS STU actively promotes academic research aimed at addressing contemporary societal and technological challenges. The university functions as a center for academic research and technological development, with emphasis on generating impactful outcomes in science and engineering domains.

Faculty members at JSS STU lead research activities across their respective disciplines, contributing to knowledge creation and innovation. Their work includes exploratory and applied research that supports academic growth and practical problem-solving.

Student Involvement in Research

Graduate students participate in research through structured coursework, projects, and dissertations. Undergraduate students are also encouraged to engage in research activities through academic projects, internships, and research fellowships, fostering early exposure to innovation and inquiry-based learning.

Research Infrastructure and Laboratories

Each academic department at JSS Science and Technology University is supported by dedicated research laboratory facilities. These laboratories are equipped with modern instruments and resources required for academic experiments, research work, and innovation-focused activities.

The availability of department-specific laboratory infrastructure ensures that both students and faculty have access to essential tools needed for research, experimentation, and technical development, supporting a conducive research environment across disciplines.

Research Output and Performance (2024)

In the year 2024, JSS Science and Technology University recorded measurable research output. The university achieved a citation count of 4208 and an H-index of 66, reflecting research impact and scholarly contribution.

During the same period, research outputs included 186 journal publications, 10 review articles, 80 conference publications, and 10 book chapters, indicating active participation in academic publishing and research dissemination.

JSS Science and Technology University is an academic institution rooted in the legacy of Sri Jayachamarajendra College of Engineering, Mysuru. The university is committed to advancing education and research in Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics, and Management through structured academic programs and research-driven initiatives.

JSS Science and Technology University provides a well-equipped campus infrastructure designed to support academic learning, teaching, and research activities. The campus includes modern facilities and resources intended to assist students and faculty in their educational pursuits.

Recognitions and Accreditations

JSS Science and Technology University is recognized by the University Grants Commission (UGC), which authorizes the university to operate as a higher education institution under national regulatory standards in India.

The university is approved by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), ensuring compliance with norms and standards prescribed for technical and professional education.

JSS Science and Technology University was established under the Karnataka Act No. 43 of 2013. The establishment and legal status of the university are notified through an official Gazette Notification of the Government of Karnataka.

The NAAC accreditation awarded to JSS Science and Technology University is valid up to July 18, 2029, reflecting the assessed quality parameters of the institution during the accreditation cycle.

Academic programmes of the university are also accredited by the National Board of Accreditation (NBA), which evaluates programme-level quality in technical education.

JSS Science and Technology University participates in the All India Survey on Higher Education (AISHE), contributing institutional data for national higher education statistics and policy planning.

The university is recognized by the Department of Scientific and Industrial Research as a Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation (DSIR-SIRO), supporting its research and innovation activities.

SJCE – Science and Technology Entrepreneurs Park (SJCE-STEP)

SJCE-STEP, also known as Sri Jayachamarajendra College of Engineering – Science and Technology Entrepreneurs Park, is a business incubation centre located in Mysuru, India. The centre focuses on supporting entrepreneurship, innovation, and startup development.

SJCE-STEP was established in the year 1985 with support from the Department of Science and Technology of the Government of India. Since its inception, the centre has played a role in promoting technology-based entrepreneurship.

Over the past three decades, SJCE-STEP has provided structured support services including training, mentoring, coaching, and consulting. These services are designed to assist students, entrepreneurs, startups, and corporate professionals in developing skills, ideas, and sustainable ventures.

SJCE-STEP contributes to the entrepreneurial ecosystem by enabling innovation-driven growth and career development through incubation support and capacity-building initiatives.

Student Cultural Activities and JAYCIANA

JSS Science and Technology University emphasizes holistic student development through academic, cultural, and extracurricular activities. The university encourages student participation in events that promote creativity, collaboration, and personal expression.

JAYCIANA is recognized as a prominent cultural event in Mysuru and serves as a platform for students to showcase talents across diverse art forms. Students from different parts of the state participate in the event through performances and cultural presentations.

Injection Molding Facility

The Central Instrumentation Facility houses an automatic injection molding machine used for manufacturing plastic components through the injection molding process. This process involves injecting molten plastic into a mold cavity where it cools and solidifies into the desired shape.

The automatic injection molding machine performs material feeding, melting, injection, cooling, and part ejection with minimal human intervention. This automation improves production efficiency and supports experimental and applied research in polymer processing and materials engineering.

Scanning Electron Microscope (SEM)

The Central Instrumentation Facility includes a Scanning Electron Microscope used for high-resolution surface imaging at the micro and nanoscale. The instrument operates by scanning a focused electron beam over the sample surface and generating signals that produce detailed images.

The SEM supports research applications across materials science, nanotechnology, electronics, and interdisciplinary scientific studies.

X-Ray Diffractometer (XRD)

The facility is equipped with an X-Ray Diffractometer designed for powder diffraction analysis. The instrument provides flexibility for structural and phase analysis of materials and supports a wide range of experimental conditions.

The system enables non-ambient diffraction experiments, including temperature-controlled studies, gas flow experiments, humidity-controlled environments, and measurements under vacuum or controlled pressure. These capabilities allow diverse material characterization studies.

Fourier Transform Infrared Spectrometer (FTIR)

The Central Instrumentation Facility includes a Fourier Transform Infrared Spectrometer with an Attenuated Total Reflectance accessory. This instrument is used for chemical and material analysis by measuring infrared light absorption.

The ATR accessory allows analysis of solid and liquid samples with minimal sample preparation. The system supports material identification, functional group analysis, and quality assessment in scientific and industrial research.

Rheology and Mixing Facility

The facility includes a laboratory-scale internal mixer used for testing highly viscous materials. It operates as part of a torque rheometer system and supports batch processing experiments.

The system enables measurement of torque, viscosity, and material behavior during mixing and processing. Data generated during experiments is analyzed through an integrated rheometer platform, supporting polymer, composite, and materials research.

Thermal Analysis Facilities

The Central Instrumentation Facility includes a Differential Scanning Calorimeter used to study heat flow associated with physical and chemical transitions in materials as a function of temperature. The instrument supports analysis of melting, crystallization, glass transition, and reaction behavior.

The facility also includes a Dynamic Mechanical Analyzer used to evaluate the mechanical properties of materials under varying temperature and loading conditions. These thermal and mechanical analysis tools support research in polymers, composites, electronics, and advanced materials.

Examination Structure

Examinations are conducted to assess student performance based on predefined academic parameters. These parameters evaluate knowledge, skills, aptitude, and attitude acquired through academic activities.

The examination department manages examination notifications, conduct of examinations, evaluation processes, declaration of results, and awarding of degrees.

The examination section was introduced in 2007 when Sri Jayachamarajendra College of Engineering was granted autonomy. After the establishment of JSS Science and Technology University in 2016, it was renamed as the Examinations Office.

CIE and SEE Structure

Academic assessment consists of Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) and Semester End Examination (SEE).

For regular courses, CIE carries 40 marks. Internal assessment includes two tests of 30 marks each and one event of 20 marks, totaling 80 marks, which is reduced to 40 marks for final calculation. A minimum of 50 percent of CIE (20 out of 40 marks) is required for eligibility to appear for SEE.

For integrated courses, CIE carries 40 marks. Internal assessment includes two tests and one event similar to regular courses. Laboratory assessment carries 40 marks separately. Students must secure 50 percent in both theory and laboratory components to qualify.

Overall assessment weightage is 40 percent for CIE (40 marks) and 60 percent for SEE (60 marks). Students must secure at least 20 or 21 marks in CIE and 24 or 25 marks in SEE. The total minimum passing requirement is 45 marks out of 100.

Undergraduate Academic Regulations (Recent Pattern)

For recent regulations (2022 onwards), the CIE to SEE ratio is 40:60. Earlier regulations (2016–2021) followed a 50:50 ratio.

Vertical progression rules restrict the number of backlogs. In recent regulations, students must not exceed 20 credits of backlog and cannot progress to the fourth year if first-year backlogs remain.

Parallel registration is limited to a maximum of three subjects, including CIE improvement subjects.

Supplementary semester and makeup examinations are not available in recent regulations. Repeaters examinations are permitted.

Practical examinations are applicable in recent regulations except for integrated laboratories.

The number of assessment events was reduced from five in earlier regulations to three in recent regulations.

If detained, a student may register for a maximum of 28 credits.

Postgraduate Academic Regulations (Recent Pattern)

For postgraduate programmes, the CIE to SEE ratio follows a 40:60 pattern in recent regulations.

Vertical progression is restricted to not exceeding four failed subjects (“F” grades).

Parallel registration is limited to three subjects, including CIE improvement.

Supplementary semester and makeup examinations are not available in recent regulations.

Repeaters examinations are allowed.

Practical examinations are not separately defined in recent postgraduate regulations.

If detained, a postgraduate student may register for a maximum of 28 credits.

IQAC plays a significant role in the accreditation process of the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) in India. It supports institutional compliance with quality benchmarks and accreditation standards.

IQAC is responsible for designing and developing the institution’s quality assurance framework and quality assurance policies. It conducts academic and administrative audits and implements strategies aimed at quality enhancement in teaching, learning, and assessment processes.

The cell promotes a culture of quality and continuous improvement across academic, research, administrative, and governance activities, aligning institutional practices with evolving technological developments and societal expectations.

Vision

To create a receptive atmosphere for enhanced educational quality.

Mission

To empower learners through innovative and inclusive educational practices.

To foster a dynamic environment that cultivates curiosity, critical thinking, and lifelong learning.

Objectives

To create and implement a quality assurance system that enables institutional benchmarking against predetermined criteria.

To promote a quality-conscious culture across all institutional operations through proactive stakeholder participation.

To support sustainable growth by institutionalizing quality practices and driving continuous improvement.

Functions

The Internal Quality Assurance Cell creates, designs, and applies quality benchmarks, metrics, and formats for academic and administrative operations.

It encourages professional growth and capacity building of faculty, employees, and stakeholders through structured learning opportunities.

It oversees and assesses the efficiency of institutional quality assurance initiatives, including assessments, surveys, and evaluations.

It organizes and conducts academic and administrative audits to enhance institutional efficiency.

It acts as a central coordinating body for quality-related activities and promotes best practices in teaching, research, administration, and governance.
